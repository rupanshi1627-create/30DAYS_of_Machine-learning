----Feature Scaling in Gradient Descent

----Feature scaling is the process of bringing all input features to a similar range.

In machine learning, Gradient Descent is used to minimize the cost function. However, when features have very different scales, it causes problems.

Example:
Age ranges from 18 to 60
Salary ranges from 20,000 to 1,000,000

In such cases, the feature with larger values (Salary) dominates the learning process.

This creates an elongated (oval-shaped) cost function, causing Gradient Descent to take a zig-zag path. As a result:
- Convergence becomes slow
- Updates become unstable
- The algorithm may not reach the optimal solution

After scaling:
- All features are brought to a similar range
- The cost function becomes more circular
- Gradient Descent follows a straight path
- Convergence becomes faster and stable

Common methods:
1. Min-Max Scaling:
x' = (x - min) / (max - min)

2. Standardization:
x' = (x - mean) / standard deviation

----Conclusion:
Feature scaling is essential for efficient and stable optimization in Gradient Descent.
